Cricket Rules

The Basics Rules

Cricket is a two-team sport where one side bats to score runs and the other bowls and fields to prevent them, with each team having a turn (an inning) at both batting and fielding to score the most runs.

The Pitch and Wickets

Cricket is played on an oval field with a rectangular pitch at the center.
At each end of the pitch are wickets, consisting of three stumps with
two bails balanced on top.

Batting and Fielding

  • Batting:Two batsmen are on the field, one at each wicket. The striker's goal is to hit the ball and score runs.
  • Bowling:A bowler from the fielding team delivers the ball to the striker's wicket.
  • Fielding: The other players on the bowling team are fielders, positioned to prevent runs and catch the ball.

Scoring Runs

Batsmen score one run for each time they run and swap places between the wickets. A ball hit to the boundary along the ground is worth four runs. A ball hit over the boundary without bouncing is worth six runs.

Dismissing the Batsman (Getting Out)

While the ball hit the stump it's out.
Fielders catch the ball means it's also out.
The wicket-keeper hits the stumps with the ball while the batsman is outside their crease and the ball isn't a wide.
