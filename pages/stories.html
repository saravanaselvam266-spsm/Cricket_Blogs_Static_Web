Important Story

Two key cricket stories are the legendary careers of Sachin Tendulkar and M.S. Dhoni, and India’s 1983 World Cup win, which boosted cricket’s popularity and paved the way for a golden era with stars like Kapil Dev, Sunil Gavaskar, Tendulkar, and Dhoni.`

A Historic Moment:

India’s first World Cup win in 1983 under Kapil Dev was a historic turning point in cricket.

A Lasting Legacy:

This victory established India as a cricket powerhouse and inspired future legends like Sunil Gavaskar and Sachin Tendulkar.

Sachin Tendulkar's "Master Blaster" Career

Sachin Tendulkar, the “Master Blaster,” is a global cricket icon. He was the first to score 11,000 ODI runs and 100 international centuries. His dream came true in 2011 when India won the World Cup after 28 years.

M.S. Dhoni: The "Captain Cool"

M.S. Dhoni, from small-town boy to cricket legend, led India to the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 ODI World Cup, and 2013 Champions Trophy. Known as “Captain Cool,” his calm leadership and innovation made him one of cricket’s greatest captains.
